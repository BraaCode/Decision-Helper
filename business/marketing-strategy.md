# Qarrar — Marketing Strategy

## 1. Positioning

**For** Arabic-speaking teams and decision-makers who are tired of opaque, untraceable decisions made in chat threads and spreadsheets,
**Qarrar is** the Arabic-first decision-intelligence platform
**that** turns any decision into a structured, weighted, and one-way-finalized record with a full audit trail.
**Unlike** spreadsheets and English-only tools, Qarrar is built RTL-native with governance-grade traceability and an AI advisor that suggests criteria and flags bias.

**One-line pitch (Arabic):**  
قرار — منصة عربية لاتخاذ قرارات شفافة وقابلة للتدقيق.

**One-line pitch (English):**  
Qarrar — where Arabic-speaking teams make decisions they can defend.

## 2. Target Personas

### Primary: "The Overwhelmed Manager" (Tariq)
- 35–50, department head in a Saudi/UAE SMB (50–500 employees)
- Makes 5–15 significant decisions per quarter (hiring, vendor, project priority)
- Pain: decisions happen in WhatsApp/Slack, no record of *why*, hard to defend in board reviews
- Budget authority: can expense a $20–50/month team tool without procurement approval

### Secondary: "The Compliance Officer" (Noura)
- 30–45, GRC or HR-compliance role in a regulated industry
- Pain: needs an auditable trail of how hiring and vendor decisions were made
- Motivated by: finalize-lock, audit log, PDF rationale export
- Buys via: enterprise contract, championed by Tariq

### Tertiary: "The Solo Consultant" (Amir)
- 28–40, independent strategy/HR consultant serving multiple clients
- Pain: needs a repeatable framework and branded export to look professional
- Buys: Pro tier, $9–19/month

## 3. Brand Voice

- **Tone:** Confident, clear, professional — not playful. Decisions are serious.
- **Language:** Arabic-first. All marketing collateral ships in Arabic, with English as a secondary translation — never the other way around.
- **Visual identity:** Deep teal + warm sand neutrals (no purple), Arabic calligraphy-inspired logo mark, generous spacing for RTL readability.
- **Proof over promise:** Lead with case studies and before/after decision stories, not feature lists.

## 5. Content Strategy

### Pillars
1. **Decision frameworks** — "How to structure a hiring decision in 6 criteria" (educational, SEO-anchored)
2. **Bias & governance** — "Why your last vendor pick was anchored to the first quote" (thought leadership)
3. **Customer stories** — "How [Company] cut hiring-decision time from 3 weeks to 4 days"
4. **Product deep-dives** — Short videos on finalize-lock, audit log, AI criteria suggestions

### Cadence
- 2 LinkedIn posts/week (Arabic)
- 1 YouTube video/month
- 1 long-form blog post/month (bilingual, Arabic primary)
- 1 customer case study/quarter

### Arabic SEO target keywords (examples)
- "أداة اتخاذ القرار" (decision-making tool)
- "مصفوفة القرار المرجح" (weighted decision matrix)
- "تدقيق القرارات الإدارية" (auditing management decisions)
- "كيف تختار أفضل موظف" (how to choose the best employee)

## 6. Launch Plan

### Pre-launch (Weeks −4 to 0)
- Recruit 10 design partners from LinkedIn network
- Publish 3 foundational blog posts for SEO seeding
- Tease on Arabic tech Twitter/Telegram with short demo clips
- Build a waitlist page with early-access incentive (3 months free Team tier)

### Launch week
- Coordinated LinkedIn posts from founders + design partners
- Product Hunt launch (secondary, English summary)
- Press outreach: Wamda, Magnitt, Arabia Tech newsletters
- YouTube launch video: "نحن نصنع القرار بشكل خاطئ — وهذا الحل" (We make decisions wrong — here's the fix)

### Post-launch (Months 1–6)
- Weekly customer-story spotlight
- Monthly webinar: "قرار الشهر" (Decision of the Month) — walk through a real decision in Qarrar
- Retargeting ads on LinkedIn (decision-making, HR, procurement audiences)

## 7. Acquisition Funnel

```
Awareness (LinkedIn / SEO / YouTube / word-of-mouth)
   → Visit (Arabic-first landing page, social proof, demo video)
      → Signup (free Pro trial, no credit card)
         → Activation (create first decision within 24h)
            → Value moment (finalize first decision + export PDF)
               → Conversion (upgrade to Team for collaboration)
                  → Expansion (invite teammates, hit seat count)
                     → Retention (audit log becomes indispensable)
```

**Activation target:** 60% of signups create a decision in session 1.  
**Conversion target:** 8% of activated free users convert to paid within 30 days.  
**Net revenue retention target:** 115% (seat expansion within teams).

## 8. Pricing Levers in Marketing

- **Free tier as a wedge:** Solo use, 3 active decisions, full RTL — removes friction, builds habit.
- **Team tier as the money-maker:** Marketed via the "you can't defend a decision that lives in WhatsApp" angle.
- **Enterprise as the credibility marker:** Even if few buy it, having the tier signals seriousness.
